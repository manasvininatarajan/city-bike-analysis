01

Daily Ridership

Volume peaked on December 18 (105,312 rides) — the system's busiest day of the month. Two snowstorms produced sharp dips that stand out clearly in the data.

Dec 14 · First Storm
24,146 rides
NYC's first significant snowfall of the season — 2.9" in Central Park, 4.6" at JFK. Snow fell through Sunday morning with freezing temperatures following.
Dec 27 · Major Blizzard
12,855 rides
NYC's biggest snowstorm in nearly 4 years — 4.3" in Central Park, state of emergency declared. NYC Emergency Management issued a travel advisory.

The two storm days account for the month's most dramatic dips — Dec 27 saw 88% fewer rides than the Dec 18 peak. Outside of weather events, ridership is remarkably steady at 60–105K per day.

02

When People Ride

A textbook commuter double-peak: 8am (149K rides) and 5pm (179K rides). The system is largely quiet before 6am and winds down after 8pm — a profile driven almost entirely by commuters rather than leisure riders.

5pm is 23× busier than 3am. The asymmetry between the morning and evening peaks — evening is notably taller — may reflect e-bike users choosing to ride home even in the cold.

03

How Long Rides Last

The median trip is just 7.5 minutes — shorter than the average (10.1 min), which is pulled up by a long tail of 30+ minute rides. Over half of all trips are under 10 minutes, consistent with short point-to-point urban hops rather than recreational cycling.

Citi Bike in December is largely a "last mile" tool — a connector, not a destination. The 30+ minute cohort (70K rides) likely represents leisure use by the small casual rider segment braving the cold.

04

Rider Mix & Bike Type

Annual members account for 89.6% of all December rides. E-bikes make up 72.7% of trips — the motor assist likely makes cold-weather commuting viable for riders who would otherwise skip the ride.

Cold weather is a natural filter: casual and tourist riders largely disappear, leaving a core of committed members. Of those members, nearly 3 in 4 chose the e-bike — electric assist may be what keeps December ridership as high as it is.

05

The busiest stations cluster around Chelsea and Midtown. The most-traveled routes are a pair of reciprocal Long Island City connections — riders going both ways between the same two stations — suggesting a tight local commuter loop in that neighborhood.

Data source: Citi Bike System Data, December 2025 — three CSV files totaling ~2.09M records after filtering trips with zero or negative duration, or trips exceeding 180 minutes (likely undocked bikes).
Methodology: Files were merged in Python using the csv module. Duration was computed as the difference between started_at and ended_at timestamps. Same-station trips were excluded from route analysis. Anomaly dates (Dec 14, Dec 27) were cross-referenced against NYC Emergency Management press releases and verified news coverage.
Weather sources: NYC.gov/em press releases (Dec 12 & Dec 24, 2025), Spectrum News NY1 (Dec 14), ABC7/WABC and CBS News NY (Dec 27).
Tools: Python (data processing & aggregation) · Chart.js 4.4 (visualizations) · HTML/CSS (report layout).
Note: This is a single-month descriptive analysis. No comparisons to prior years are made; findings reflect December 2025 conditions only.
